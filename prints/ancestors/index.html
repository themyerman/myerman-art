Ancestors

A modern Native boy stands at the edge of a lake, looking down. In the water, looking back up at him: many ancestors — dressed in regalia from many nations, many eras. The mountains behind him are permanent, indifferent, solid. He carries all of this. Loss and memory are not separate things; they are the same water.
