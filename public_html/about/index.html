About

I make digital art at the intersection of two worlds I've always lived in.

My ancestry is Hodinǫ̱hsǫ́:nih (Haudenosaunee / Iroquois Confederacy) and Ngäbe-Buglé from Panama. My childhood was hair metal on the radio, Dungeons & Dragons on the kitchen table, and Indiana Jones on the big screen. All of that is in the work.

I draw from Indigenous tradition, political urgency, pop culture nostalgia, and whatever's making me angry or hopeful on any given week. The pieces that sell at art markets are the ones people stop and stare at — the ones that feel familiar and completely foreign at the same time.

I work entirely digitally, and I care a lot about resolution and print quality. Every piece in the shop is available as a high-quality giclée poster, stretched canvas, metal print, or acrylic face-mount — printed to order, shipped worldwide.
